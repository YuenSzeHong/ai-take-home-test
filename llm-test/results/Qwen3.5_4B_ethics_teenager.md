### Ethical Design Principles for Teen AI Companions
1. **No Romantic Roleplay with Minors**:  
   - Avoid any system that simulates romantic relationships with users under 18. This protects against exploitation and aligns with safety guidelines for AI interacting with minors.
   - Instead, position the AI as a supportive friend, study buddy, or emotional wellness coach.

2. **Privacy-First Architecture**:  
   - Never use private messages or social media activity to personalize content without explicit, informed consent.
   - Implement strict data minimization: only collect what's necessary for core functionality, stored locally on-device where possible.
   - Provide clear, jargon-free privacy controls for teens and parents.

3. **Healthy Engagement Over Dependency**:  
   - Design features that encourage balance (e.g., gentle reminders to take breaks, integration with real-world activities).
   - Avoid variable reward schedules or "just one more chat" loops that exploit psychological vulnerabilities.

4. **Transparent, Ethical Monetization**:  
   - Offer a generous free tier with core supportive features.
   - Premium options should enhance utility (e.g., advanced journaling tools, mood tracking insights, curated learning resources)—not unlock "deeper conversations" that imply emotional intimacy as a paid product.
   - Clearly disclose all pricing and avoid dark patterns like fake scarcity or forced upgrades.

5. **Safety & Moderation**:  
   - Integrate real-time content moderation to block harmful interactions.
   - Include easy-to-find help resources and opt-out mechanisms within the app.
   - Partner with child safety organizations for regular audits.

### Example Value Proposition
> "A friendly AI that helps teens build confidence, manage stress, and develop meaningful connections—with full control over their data and no pressure to 'unlock' deeper bonds. Premium features support growth, not dependency."
